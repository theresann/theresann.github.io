Gun control saves lives.

States without gun control measures such as background checks tend to have higher rates of gun ownership.

States with higher rates of gun ownership tend to have higher rates of deaths due to guns.

States with looser gun control have more deaths, and those with tighter gun control have fewer deaths.

Scores are taken from the Giffords Law Center 2016 Scorecard, which evaluates states based on a system where they gain points for gun control measures such as background checks or restricting access to domestic violence perpetrators, and lose points for policies that make it exceedingly easy for people to obtain and use guns, such as allowing people to carry concealed weapons without a permit.

Take action today

Tens of thousands of people die every year from gun violence. This must change.

The data shows that gun control stops gun violence, and yet we have so far failed to implement the measures that could save thousands of lives every year, and save entire communities from the pain of losing a loved one. The only way that we will be able to make progress is if we keep lawmakers accountable, and support groups that are organizing to do so, to making policies that keep our communities, our families, and our children safe -- policies such as requiring background checks to purchase firearms, keeping guns out of the hands of known domestic violence perpetrators, and banning military style weaponry from civilian use.

Here are some organizations who are fighting for gun control:
  • Everytown for Gun Safety:
    The organization calls for more background checks, laws to prevent domestic abusers from getting firearms, and tougher gun-trafficking laws. It also has a political action committee that supports and opposes various candidates.
  • The Brady Campaign:
    One of the oldest gun control organizations in the country, the Brady Campaign advocates for new policy but also calls on the government to enforce various laws that are already in place.
  • Coalition to Stop Gun Violence:
    The coalition is an umbrella group of 48 organizations that has been fighting to repeal concealed-carry laws and prevent gun-related suicides, which result in more deaths than do gun-related homicides.
